NoddyTales v0.9.3 · b44 BEFORE proof — mood_throughline "-ly" artifact

Defect: src/engine-v2.js line 5779 (a b42 mood-as-driver variant)
  '[name:{protagonist.name}] folded their arms [c:{mood_throughline.text}]-ly. ...'

The beat appends a literal "-ly" to the mood token. mood_throughline.text is an
ADJECTIVE or MULTI-WORD PHRASE, not a verb stem, so the suffix produces broken
adverbs.

Reproduction: 95 / 2100 forced-mood samples (7 moods × 300 reps, age 9) rendered
an "[a-z]-ly" artifact. Detector regex: /[a-z]-ly\b/i.

Rendered examples:
  • Cole folded their arms clumsy-ly.
  • Cole folded their arms legendary-ly.
  • Cole folded their arms puzzled-ly.
  • Cole folded their arms thoughtfully menacing-ly.
  • Cole folded their arms professionally unhinged-ly.
  • Cole folded their arms deeply over it-ly.

Root cause: the variant was authored assuming mood is an adverb-able stem. It is
not — moods are adjectives/phrases ("clumsy", "professionally unhinged", "deeply
over it"). Fix: drop the "-ly" suffix; use a construction that takes an adjective
naturally ("folded their arms, looking [mood]").
