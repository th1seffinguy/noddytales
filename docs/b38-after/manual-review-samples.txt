=== 5 KID samples (ages 6-7) ===

--- kid age 6 (show_wrong_v3, color=apple red) ---
Title: The Day Cole Saved the Show
 The whole thing rested on one wobbly telescope. Cole had practiced. The cat had practiced. The bedroom was, technically, ready.
 The cat blanked. The wobbly telescope hit the floor a second later. Two crises at once.
 Out came the only word that fit: "plop." Cole zoomed for emphasis. The bit was a different bit now. Underneath everything, Cole was running on pure sleepy. Somebody had brought pizza. Nobody knew when. Nobody minded. Off to the side, the dragon was processing all of this with visible difficulty.
 Cole pointed at the broken wobbly telescope: "plop." The wobbly telescope reassembled itself in roughly the wrong order. The cat applauded the wrong order. The show was now better. The ceiling flashed apple red for exactly two seconds. "glorp," went somebody. Nobody asked who.
 Backstage, Cole and the cat looked at each other and at the exact same moment said "plop." That was the show's name now, apparently. Forever.

--- kid age 7 (rule_loophole_v3, color=electric blue) ---
Title: Cole and the Dragon's Rule
 It was a normal day at the bedroom. Cole and the cat were minding their own business. The day was, technically, fine. So far.
 Off-limits, said the dragon, pointing at the pizza. Cole turned a particular shade of sleepy. The dragon had not seen that energy before.
 Cole zoomed sideways while holding the tiny key. This was a different move than the one the rule said no to. A different move! The rule said nothing back.
 "snorble!" said Cole. The cat nodded. Three witnesses appeared from nowhere. They also nodded. The dragon tried to recount. The recount confirmed the loophole. Cole's sleeves turned electric blue. Nobody explained this.
 The cat watched Cole yawn and yawned too. "glorp," said Cole, using it for the last time today. The cat approved. Sleep.

 Cole and the cat had arrived at the bedroom with a simple plan: have a regular afternoon. The cat was good at regular afternoons.

 Cole zoomed sideways while holding the tiny key. This was a different move than the one the rule said no to. A different move! The rule said nothing back.

--- kid age 7 (goal_spine_v3, color=midnight blue) ---
Title: How Cole Tried to Win the Official Tiny Trophy
 Cole woke up with one plan: win the official tiny trophy, today at the bedroom. The cat was already in.
 The dragon stood between Cole and victory. Cole felt sleepy about it. The dragon did not look like it was going to move on its own.
 Cole waved a flag — bright midnight blue — at the dragon, who had not been briefed on any flag and froze just long enough. Cole zoomed briefly, then pretended that had not happened. Somebody had brought pizza. Nobody knew when. Nobody minded.
 Cole said "bonk" once. The dragon found somewhere else to be and Cole won the tiny trophy through the gap with the cat.
 Cole climbed into bed. The cat climbed in too. The day got its closing line: "glorp," whispered Cole. The cat sneezed in approval.

--- kid age 6 (lost_snack_v3, color=rainbow) ---
Title: Cole vs the Dragon
 Cole set the pizza down at the bedroom for one second. The cat watched. Cole turned to grab a napkin. Just one second.
 The pizza had vanished. Cole felt sleepy and unusually focused about it. The dragon noticed and tried to act normal. It was not working.
 The trail led to the cat. Of course. The cat had a single crumb of pizza on its face. "YOU?" said Cole. The cat looked away politely. There was a small zoomed moment that nobody quite witnessed in full. A tiny spot of rainbow appeared on the back of Cole's hand. Blinked once. Gone. The radiator said "glorp," which radiators do not usually do.
 Cole tried "flumpy" one more time. The dragon's elaborate hiding spot collapsed in three pieces. The pizza rolled out, blinking in the light.
 The next morning at breakfast, Cole's mom asked what was for snack. "flumpy crackers," said Cole, totally normal. Mom nodded. The cat approved. It was just a word that meant something now.


=== 5 TWEEN samples (ages 11-13) ===

--- tween age 11 (show_wrong_v3, color=apple red) ---
Title: Cole's Big Show
 Cole had been workshopping the pickle jar thing all week — the cat was reluctantly co-starring at the bedroom.
 The pickle jar broke. Cole held half a pickle jar and made eye contact with the universe.
 The pickle jar failed exactly on cue. Cole zoomed across the stage like that had been the plan all along. The audience was, technically, into it. Mostly.
 The dragon critiqued the show out loud. The cat glared. Cole absorbed the feedback in real time and kept going. The bit improved. Cole noticed a streak of apple red across the wall. Or did they. Just then a "glorp" cracked the silence. Nobody admitted to it. Off to the side, a pizza sat there, mostly forgotten, definitely still part of the day.
 Cole pointed at the broken pickle jar: "fwoosh." The pickle jar reassembled itself in roughly the wrong order. The cat applauded the wrong order. The show was now better. The whole day had a sleepy energy to it. Nobody could explain why.
 Walking out, Cole was already rewriting the encore. "fwoosh," they said with the cat, like a band name. Apparently they had a band.

--- tween age 12 (rule_loophole_v3, color=electric blue) ---
Title: The Loophole at the Bedroom
 Over by the bedroom, Cole was doing the bare minimum. So was the cat. Suspiciously calm energy. Things were technically fine. They would not stay fine.
 Off-limits, said the dragon, pointing at the pizza. Cole turned a particular shade of sleepy. The dragon had not seen that energy before.
 Cole held up the pocket-sized door like a permit. The angle was specific. The angle was correct. The rule, as written, had nothing to say about the angle. Cole simply executed. Somewhere in there, Cole zoomed for emphasis. A "glorp" hung in the air for a second longer than expected.
 The dragon watched Cole hold the pocket-sized door and felt outmaneuvered by precisely one inch of vocabulary. Bureaucracy is just words. Cole had the right ones. Cole's sleeves turned electric blue. Nobody explained this. A wet sneakers smell rolled through the room and rolled back out.
 "grumblepoof," said Cole. The rule, which had said "no pizza," now apparently said "yes pizza." The dragon checked the paperwork. The paperwork agreed.
 On the walk home, Cole filed the loophole for future use. The cat was definitely going to use it back on Cole eventually. "grumblepoof," said Cole one more time, quietly. The cat saved it.

--- tween age 13 (goal_spine_v3, color=tomato red) ---
Title: Cole vs the Dragon
 Decision made. Today, at the bedroom, Cole would win the official tiny trophy. The cat was on board. Quietly. Without making it a thing.
 The dragon stood between Cole and victory. Cole felt sleepy about it. The dragon did not look like it was going to move on its own.
 Cole zoomed like a professional who had committed to a specific kind of unhinged. The dragon had not budgeted for this. Cole did not break eye contact. For two seconds the lamp glowed tomato red. Then back to normal. A "glorp" came out of the closet. The closet stayed closed.
 The dragon picked up the pizza, examined it like it was an artifact, and put it back upside down. Cole said absolutely nothing. The silence was the whole bit.
 "squonk!" yelled Cole. The dragon slid aside and Cole won the tiny trophy — the cat gave one dignified nod.
 In bed that night, Cole catalogued the day. The cat was already asleep, technically a co-conspirator. "squonk," said Cole, one last time. Quietly, but on the record.

--- tween age 11 (lost_snack_v3, color=midnight blue) ---
Title: The Day the Pizza Vanished
 One thing mattered today: the pizza at the bedroom. The cat was the witness.
 The pizza had vanished. Cole got sleepy about it. The dragon tried a slow shrug. The shrug arrived late and crooked.
 Cole zoomed past the dragon with theatrical timing. None of it was on purpose. All of it landed.
 The real culprit, obviously, was the cat. Cole should have known. The cat had crumbs of pizza on its face and zero regret in its eyes.
 "zoinks!" said Cole. The dragon froze mid-bite and the pizza dropped out of its mouth, landing perfectly on Cole's plate. Case closed by gravity. Cole's shoes briefly turned midnight blue. Briefly.
 Later, in the group chat, somebody typed "glorp" with no context. Three people reacted. Two of them weren't even there today. The word was officially out. Cole put the phone down.
