# Story Quality Audit — v2.7.0 post-change audit

Reproducible audit pack. Generated by `scripts/audit-stories.js`.
Ages 2/4/6/8/10/12 × 5 stories each = 30 stories total.
Ages 6+ route through v3 (random blueprint per story). Picks vary deterministically per sample index.

Scoring rubric (1-5):
- **Humor** — does this make a kid laugh?
- **Substance** — is there a real arc (setup → problem → action → payoff)?
- **Kid agency** — is the kid the driver, not the witness?
- **Choice integration** — are chosen words load-bearing or decoration?
- **Rereadability** — would the kid ask for "again"?
- **Age fit** — vocabulary and complexity right for the age?

| Tier | Humor | Substance | Kid agency | Choice integration | Rereadability | Age fit | Δ |

| Little (age 4) | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 4 | — |
| Kid (age 6) — v3 | **4** | **4** | 3 | **4** | **4** | 4 | +3 |

| Tween (age 12) — v3 | 3 | **4** | 3 | **4** | 3 | 3 | +3 |

**What measurably improved (v3 ages 6-13):**

1. **goal_spine_v3 now states the goal.** Pre: "today was the day. No matter what." Post: "The plan was simple: find the way home." / "Today, at the diner, Cole was going to win the silly race." Goal is named in P1, referenced by the obstacle in P2, and cashed in the P5 payoff ("Cole found the way home despite the troll"). Title patterns also use `{goal.titleText}` so titles like *"The Day Cole Found the Way Home"* / *"How Cole Tried to Build the Perfect Hideout"* sell the story before the reader starts.

2. **lost_snack reveals are funnier and more varied.** Three new culprit-reveal beats: ally burps and crumbs come out / ally finds something fascinating on the ceiling / ally holds a fake leaf and gets caught. Color is now a clue ("a midnight blue smudge on the floor right where the llama had been sitting") instead of decoration.

3. **show_wrong has visually-silly prop disasters.** Pre: "The library card broke in half." Post: "The prop launched itself off the table, bounced once, and landed in the [ally]'s lap" / "The [prop] made a noise it should not have been able to make. Then it tipped sideways. Then it kept tipping. It was still tipping." Vivid kid-readable physical comedy.

4. **rule_loophole rules are absurd but specific.** Pre: "Nobody touches the [food]." Post: "[food] can only be touched on Tuesdays" / "Nobody is allowed to eat [food] while standing" / "[food] must remain at least three feet from any [protagonist]." Each rule gives the loophole a concrete shape to work around.

5. **Mood is now load-bearing.** New mood-as-approach beat: "Cole walked up to the troll in full dramatic mode. Not asking permission. Not apologizing. Just dramatic. The troll had not prepared for dramatic." Mood shapes the strategy instead of being a feeling label.

6. **freeword2 callback ("payoff_word") more reliable.** v2.7.0 fixed a flavor-callback bug where bare text matches blocked highlight callback emission. The flavor pass now requires a highlight TOKEN, not just bare text — so when freeword2 coincidentally appears bare in goal text, the callback still fires to ensure it lands as a highlight.

**What didn't change (intentionally):**

- Tot (ages 2-3) and little (age 4) — kept stable per spec ("Keep v2 ages 2-5 stable unless obvious low-risk improvements emerge"). Content already passes acceptance.
- Tween-specific voice — added one show_wrong_v3 tween escalation variant and one rule_loophole_v3 tween problem variant, but a deeper tween-voice pass remains for a future build.
- Endings — left mostly as-is; the bedtime/anytime split from v2.6.2 already gives 2 ending modes.

**Remaining gaps (recommended next-step work):**

- More variety in lost_snack twist (3 new variants now; could grow to 6 for less repetition across replays).
- show_wrong improvisation beats could lean harder into the chosen `move` (e.g., the move IS the saving improv).
- Tween still under-served vs kid/big — needs its own beat-author pass for distinct voice.
- Tot/little could get the same goal-aware treatment if a v2 goal slot were added there.
