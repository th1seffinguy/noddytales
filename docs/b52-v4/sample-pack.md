**Cole and the Pancake Flip**

> Saturday morning. Pancake morning. Cole got the bowl, and the puppy got the spoon, because the puppy always gets the spoon.
>
> Cole flipped the first pancake. It flew up... and landed right on the puppy's head. "boing!" said Cole. The puppy sat very still, wearing the pancake like a hat.
>
> Cole flipped the second pancake. It flew up... and stuck to the ceiling. Cole looked at the puppy. The puppy looked at the ceiling. They waited. Nothing.
>
> Then Cole had an idea. Cole held a plate way up high, like a catcher. One... two... "boing!" The pancake came down — right onto the plate. Perfect.
>
> So Cole ate the plate pancake, and the puppy ate the hat pancake. Then it was time for bed, and Cole fell asleep still a little bit proud. Goodnight, pancake catcher.

**The Puppy Hiccups**

> The puppy had the hiccups. Big ones. "boing!" went the puppy, every few seconds. "boing!" Cole decided to help.
>
> Cure number one: holding your breath with puffed-up cheeks. The puppy puffed. Cole counted to ten. "boing!" Nope.
>
> Cure number two: standing very, very still, like a statue. The puppy froze. Cole froze too, just to be fair. They were both excellent statues. "boing!" Still nope.
>
> So Cole tiptoed away... tiptoed back... and yelled "BOO!" The puppy jumped. Then it blinked. Then it waited. No hiccup. NO HICCUP! The cure worked!
>
> Then Cole hiccuped. A big one. The puppy tiptoed away... tiptoed back... "BOO!" Cole jumped, and laughed, and the hiccup was gone. Fair is fair.
>
> They shook on it: whoever hiccups next gets the boo. The puppy kept one eye on Cole all afternoon, just in case.

## v4_kid_backwards_day  ·  kid  ·  bedtime

**Backwards Day**

> At breakfast, Cole made an announcement: today was Backwards Day. Dessert first. Pajamas to the table. Walking in reverse. The overconfident raccoon was appointed judge, because the judge cannot also be a player. The judge accepted, snacks included.
>
> It started small. Cole said "goodnight" instead of "good morning." Wore the backpack on the front. Read a comic from the last page to the first, which honestly made it funnier. The judge awarded full points.
>
> Then it escalated, as Backwards Days do. Backwards walking down the hallway: points. Backwards hopping in the yard: many points. Backwards skipping past the garden — right toward the sprinkler that nobody remembered was on. The judge saw it coming. The judge said nothing. Judges are like that.
>
> SPLOOSH. Cole stood in the spray, fully soaked, fully backwards, and ruled it on the spot: "Ten out of ten." The judge agreed by running through the sprinkler too — forwards, because rules only applied to players. They went around twice more, both directions, until everyone was soaked and the scores stopped mattering.
>
> Backwards Day ended the only way it could: dinner after dessert, bath after the sprinkler had already done most of the work, and then bed — because Cole ruled that sleep is the one thing better forwards. "Good morning," said Cole, turning out the light, and fell asleep facing exactly the right way. Perfect score.
